Dear Concheros & Concheras,

If you’re reading this it means that you’ve been officially selected to partake in the 2nd rendition of Burning Conch. If it’s your second Burn, congratulations; this must mean that you didn’t mess up the first time around and have been invited back. If it’s your first time… well… good luck. In a few weeks you’ll be arriving in the Turks and Caicos for Burning Conch 2020. Here is a little intro into what you should expect, what you should bring and some tips on how to make the most out of the 10 days we’ll be spending together.

Location

You’ll be staying in the newly built (almost completed) ‘Yellow House’ on the property of Sunshine Nursery (Our family business) in Grace Bay, Providenciales. We are walking distance from Grace Bay Beach and the various hotels and restaurants in this ‘happening’ part of the island. We will likely be spending quite a bit of time chilling in and around the new casa - our headquarters - when we are not partaking in some of the adventures we have planned for you.

Arrivals

Most of you will be arriving on March 6th on an Air Canada flight. In order to best arrange pickups and drop offs we ask that you fill in your flight information here.

You will be given an immigration form on your flight. You can fill out your arrival address as follows: The Yellow House, 36 Garden Loop, Grace Bay, Providenciales.

What to bring?

Island clothing, one sweater for windy/chilly days, 1 pair of active shoes, 1 pear of flip flops, Sunscreen, 1 litre of alcohol each (mandatory), Bed Sheets, Pillow (if you can), Carne Argentina, Rolling Papers, Mask and Snorkel (for those who want to snorkel), 1 Towel, Boules de pétanque, salsa picante.

Something to share - whether it be food, a game, a treat, magic mushrooms, or a great playlist.

What to expect?

A lot of sun, a lot of ocean, boat trips, some yoga, spliffs, cerveza, mosquitos, getting yelled at by Mike Giese, painting, pétanque, pétards, barbeques, music, beach, kekekeke.

Rules

We don’t have too many of these. Except for the basic shit - don’t be an Asshole. On day 1 we’ll give you guys a bit of an orientation so that you stay out of trouble and don’t end up sleeping on the streets, or even worse, with Mike.
